Why Breathwork Matters

Breathwork is the active practice of consciously controlling your breathing rate and depth. It acts as a direct remote control for your autonomic nervous system. By intentionally changing how you breathe, you can actively reduce stress, lower your heart rate, improve focus, and signal your brain to enter a deep state of relaxation.

Breathing Patterns Explained

Box Breathing

Pattern: Inhale (4s) • Hold (4s) • Exhale (4s) • Hold (4s)
Best for: Grounding, focus, and rapid stress reduction. Often used by first responders and athletes to clear the mind and steady the heart.

Paced Breathing

Pattern: Inhale (4s) • Hold (1s) • Exhale (6s) • Hold (1s)
Best for: Deep relaxation and preparing for sleep. Extending the exhale longer than the inhale specifically triggers the parasympathetic "rest and digest" response.

Physiological Sigh

Pattern: Inhale (7s) • Hold (1s) • Top-off Inhale (2s) • Exhale (4s) • Hold (2.5s)
Best for: Real-time anxiety relief. The "double inhale" reinflates collapsed alveoli (air sacs) in the lungs, allowing your body to efficiently offload built-up carbon dioxide.

Alternate Nostril

Pattern: Inhale Left (4s) • Hold (4s) • Exhale Right (4s) • Hold (4s)
Best for: Balancing energy and centering the mind. Traditionally used in yoga to harmonize the left and right hemispheres of the brain.

Timer Controls
